# Phase 14 — Stewardship, Sustainability, and Long-Term Human Alignment (Governance Pack v1)

## Purpose

This document converts the Phase 14 vision into an actionable governance starter
kit. It formalizes the **Institutional Stewardship Model**, the **Global
Charter**, and the **Safety & Ethics Policy** so Infæmous Freight can remain
aligned, accountable, and resilient over multiple generations.

---

## 1) Institutional Stewardship Model (Dual Structure)

### 1.1 Entities & Roles

| Entity                                | Role                                                     | Authority                                              |
| ------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Infæmous Freight Corp**             | Operates, builds, sells, and scales the platform.        | Operational execution and commercial growth.           |
| **Infæmous Freight Trust/Foundation** | Oversees ethics, alignment, and public-good obligations. | Veto rights on unsafe changes and mandate enforcement. |

### 1.2 Trust/Foundation Mandate

The Trust/Foundation exists to **protect long-term alignment** and prevent
short‑term incentives from eroding public trust. It must:

- Maintain the Global Charter and ensure compliance.
- Exercise veto rights on high‑risk AI or governance changes.
- Sponsor independent audits and publish transparency reports.
- Convene emergency governance councils during systemic risk events.

### 1.3 Governance Mechanics

- **Board Composition:** Balanced representation from technical, ethics,
  operations, and public-interest stakeholders.
- **Veto Triggers:** Material changes to AI safety policies, model deployment
  scopes, or override mechanisms.
- **Transparency Guarantees:** Annual charter compliance report and public audit
  summary.

---

## 2) Infæmous Freight Global Charter (v1)

### 2.1 What the AI Is Allowed to Do

- Optimize freight routing, scheduling, pricing, and supply coordination
  **within approved safety and fairness constraints**.
- Provide explainable recommendations that reference policy and confidence
  levels.
- Operate within authorized geographic and regulatory boundaries.

### 2.2 What the AI Will Never Do

- Self-expand authority or control beyond explicitly granted scopes.
- Modify or disable its own safety constraints or human override mechanisms.
- Deploy globally without governance and regulatory approval.

### 2.3 How It Can Be Challenged

- **Human challenge pathway:** regulators, operators, or the Trust may request
  review.
- **Public accountability:** published summaries of escalations and outcomes.
- **Independent audit channel:** external auditors can request evidence
  packages.

### 2.4 Oversight & Accountability

- The Trust/Foundation maintains ultimate oversight.
- The operating company publishes decision transparency metrics quarterly.
- Material policy changes require multi‑party approval (Trust + executive
  governance + safety council).

## 3) Long-Term AI Alignment Framework

### 3.1 Alignment Layers

1. **Technical alignment** — prevent harmful actions and unsafe system behavior.
2. **Economic alignment** — prevent exploitative optimization or market abuse.
3. **Social alignment** — avoid destabilizing societal impacts.
4. **Civilizational alignment** — prevent systemic harms over multi‑decade
   timelines.

### 3.2 Continuous Alignment Loop

```
Human values
   ↓
AI policy update
   ↓
AI behavior
   ↓
Monitoring & audits
   ↓
Human review
   ↓
Policy refinement
```

### 3.3 Implementation Requirements

- Maintain versioned policy artifacts and change histories.
- Establish a recurring **alignment review cadence** (minimum quarterly).
- Codify red‑line constraints with immutable enforcement rules.

---

## 4) Self‑Limiting Power Controls

### 4.1 Hard Limits

- AI cannot self‑expand authority.
- AI cannot rewrite safety rules or override mechanisms.
- AI cannot bypass human approvals.
- AI cannot deploy globally without governance approval.

### 4.2 Enforcement Mechanisms

- Immutable guardrails stored outside the AI runtime.
- Dual‑control approvals for high‑risk actions.
- Automated rollback for policy violations or safety regressions.

---

## 5) Transparency & Explainability at Scale

### 5.1 Required Outputs Per Major Decision

- Decision trace
- Confidence score
- Policy reference
- Human‑readable rationale

### 5.2 Auditability Standards

- All major decisions must be **reproducible** and **legally defensible**.
- Maintain evidence packages for regulators, partners, and auditors.
